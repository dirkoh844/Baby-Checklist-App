Warning signs When to call · works offline

Whoever you call, whoever picks up, say this first:

"I gave birth on [date]."

Pregnancy complications kill women up to a year after birth, and the person triaging you will not think of it unless you say it. Say it to the 911 operator, the ER desk, and every nurse.

POST · call 911 now

Call 911

AWHONN's post-birth warning signs. These apply for a full year after birth.

P

Pain in the chest

A blood clot in the lung, or a heart problem. It is not normal to have chest pain after having a baby.

O

Obstructed breathing or shortness of breath

Same causes. Do not talk yourself out of this one.

S

Seizures

Can mean eclampsia: blood pressure high enough to cause a seizure. It happens after birth, not only before.

T

Thoughts of hurting yourself or the baby

A medical emergency, and a treatable one. Call 911 or 988. You are not a bad mother for having the thought.

BIRTH · call your provider now

Do not wait for office hours. If you cannot reach anyone, go to the ER.

B

Bleeding

Soaking through more than one pad in an hour, or clots the size of an egg or bigger, or passing tissue. This is obstetric hemorrhage.

I

Incision that is not healing

Any cesarean or tear repair that opens, drains, or reddens.

R

Red or swollen leg, painful or warm to the touch

A blood clot in the leg. Do not massage it. Call.

T

Temperature of 100.4 F or higher

Or chills. Infection.

H

Headache that will not go away

Especially unrelieved by medicine, or with vision changes. Postpartum high blood pressure, which can arrive days or weeks after birth.

VBAC and after a cesarean

During labor, uterine rupture shows as: severe persistent scar pain, a sudden change in the quality of the pain, blood in the urine, the baby's head receding, an abnormal heart tracing, maternal tachycardia, contractions falling apart, or sudden vaginal bleeding. Tell the nurse immediately.

After a cesarean, the incision is the thing to watch: opening, draining, spreading redness, or a fever with it.

Who to call

911 · emergency 988 · crisis line 1-833-852-6262 · Maternal Mental Health Hotline, 24/7 1-800-944-4773 · Postpartum Support International

Thoughts of harming yourself or the baby are a symptom, not a verdict on you, and they are treatable. Make the call.

ER now, or call 911

Under 3 months, a newborn can go from fine to very sick quickly, and the early signs are quiet.

Fever

A rectal temperature of 100.4 F (38 C) or higher, under 3 months, is an emergency. Do not give fever medicine first. Do not re-check in an hour to see if it drops. Do not wait for a callback. Under 3 months, take the temperature rectally, not in the ear or armpit.

Breathing trouble

Fast breathing, grunting with each breath, nostrils flaring, skin sucking in between the ribs, or blue or gray lips, skin, or nails.

Lethargy

Hard to wake, floppy, not interacting. If the baby will not stir after being undressed and prodded, that is not a good sleeper.

Color change

Pale, mottled, gray, or blue.

Inconsolable, high-pitched crying

That cannot be soothed at all.

Call the pediatrician today

Feeding refusal

Refusing feeds, missing two feeds in a row, feeding under 5 minutes, or falling asleep before finishing every time. Newborns feed 8 to 12 times a day, roughly every 2 to 3 hours.

Too few wet diapers

After the first week, fewer than 6 wet diapers in 24 hours, or no wet diaper at all for 6 to 8 hours. The tracker counts this for you and warns.

Not back to birth weight by 2 weeks

Babies lose weight at first and should regain it by about two weeks.

Jaundice

Yellow skin or eyes. Usually shows up day 2 to 4. Yellow within the first 24 hours is urgent.

Vomiting

Forceful or projectile, green, or after every feed. Blood in vomit or stool.

Dehydration

No tears when crying, dry mouth, sunken eyes, sunken soft spot.

Before you call, have this ready: the temperature and exactly when you took it, how it was taken (rectal), any medicine given with dose and time, when the last feed was, and the wet and dirty diaper count for the last 24 hours. The tracker has the last three.
