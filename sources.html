Labor and birth preferences

  • ACOG — vaginal birth after cesarean deliveryClinical guidance for individualized TOLAC/VBAC counseling and management; access to the full bulletin may be restricted.
  • ACOG — VBAC patient FAQPatient-facing overview of candidacy, benefits, risks, induction, monitoring, and facility planning.
  • ACOG — approaches to limit intervention during labor and birthClear liquids, mobility, individualized pushing, and other lower-intervention practices when clinically appropriate.
  • ACOG — first and second stage labor managementCurrent definitions and management recommendations for labor progress and arrest.
  • ACOG — delayed umbilical cord clampingAt least 30–60 seconds for vigorous term and preterm infants.
  • WHO — caring for newbornsWarmth, rooming-in, feeding, and delaying the first bath for at least 24 hours.

Newborn and parent safety

  • CDC Hear Her — urgent maternal warning signsPregnancy and the year after delivery, including extreme swelling, headache, breathing trouble, bleeding, fever, and mental-health emergencies.
  • American Academy of Pediatrics — fever and your babyFor age 3 months or younger, a rectal temperature of 100.4 °F / 38 °C or higher requires an immediate call and evaluation.
  • U.S. CPSC — safe sleep and infant productsFirm, flat sleep surface; bare sleep space; product-safety and recall guidance.
  • NIH Safe to Sleep — supervised tummy timeStart with short, awake, watched sessions and build gradually.
  • CDC — clean, sanitize, and store feeding itemsClean after each feeding and sanitize daily for babies under 2 months, premature babies, or babies with weakened immune systems.
  • CDC — newborn breastfeeding basicsBreastfeeding frequency, early wet-diaper counts, weight regain, latch, and signs that intake needs review; other feeding plans may differ.
  • FDA — unauthorized infant vital-sign monitorsConsumer monitoring is not a substitute for supervision, safe sleep, or authorized medical care.
  • CDC — developmental milestonesAge-based checklists and guidance to act early when skills are lost or concerns arise.

Newborn prophylaxis and vaccination

  • CDC — gonococcal infections among neonatesWhy universal ocular prophylaxis is recommended and why prenatal screening remains important.
  • CDC — December 2025 hepatitis B recommendationIndividual shared decision for infants of confirmed HBsAg-negative mothers; time-critical prophylaxis remains for positive or unknown status.

Consent and Virginia rules

  • Virginia 12VAC5-20-100 — human-research consentThis is a research-subject regulation, not authority for ordinary maternity care. The old citation was removed from the plan.
  • Virginia 12VAC5-90-140 — ophthalmia neonatorumDirects the attending clinician to ensure erythromycin is administered; it does not state a parental opt-out process.
