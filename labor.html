When to head in

The 5-1-1 rule — contractions every 5 minutes, lasting 1 minute, for 1 hour. That pattern usually means active labor: call your provider and go. Your OB may give you different numbers (4-1-1 is common for second babies) — theirs win.
Call right away, any time, for: water breaking (note the time and color — green or brown means say so), heavy bleeding, severe headache or vision changes, noticeably reduced baby movement, fever, or regular contractions before 37 weeks.

General guidance, not medical advice. When unsure, call — Labor & Delivery lines answer 24/7 and would rather hear from you.

Stages, at a glance

Early labor — contractions 5–20 min apart, mild and irregular; cervix to ~6 cm. Often hours (or a day). Home is usually the place: rest, eat light, hydrate, shower.

Active labor — stronger, ~3–5 min apart, about a minute long; this is the 5-1-1 zone and hospital time.

Transition — the most intense and usually the shortest; contractions 2–3 min apart to full 10 cm.

Pushing & birth — minutes to a few hours, guided by your team.

Placenta + golden hour — delivery of the placenta, then skin-to-skin and the first feed. Say it's in your birth plan.
