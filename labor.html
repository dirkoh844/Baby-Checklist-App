When to head in

The 5-1-1 rule — when it's time to go

Contractions every 5 minutes, lasting 1 minute, for 1 hour. That pattern usually means active labor: call your provider and go. Your OB may give you different numbers (4-1-1 is common for second babies) — theirs win.

Call right away, any time — the 6 exceptions

Water breaking (note the time and color — green or brown means say so), heavy bleeding, severe headache or vision changes, noticeably reduced baby movement, fever, or regular contractions before 37 weeks.

General guidance, not medical advice. When unsure, call — Labor & Delivery lines answer 24/7 and would rather hear from you.

Stages, at a glance

1Early labor — 5–20 min apart · stay home

Contractions 5–20 min apart, mild and irregular; cervix to ~6 cm. Often hours (or a day). Home is usually the place: rest, eat light, hydrate, shower.

2Active labor — 3–5 min apart · hospital time

Stronger, ~3–5 min apart, about a minute long; this is the 5-1-1 zone and hospital time.

3Transition — 2–3 min apart · shortest, most intense

The most intense and usually the shortest; contractions 2–3 min apart to full 10 cm.

4Pushing & birth — minutes to a few hours

Minutes to a few hours, guided by your team.

5Placenta & the golden hour — skin-to-skin

Delivery of the placenta, then skin-to-skin and the first feed. Say it's in your birth plan.
